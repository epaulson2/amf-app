# AMF Synthesis Candidates
**Status:** Pending founder review — June 11, 2026
**Total ideas:** 29 (12 enhancements, 10 synthesis originals, 7 moonshots)

## Philosophy

The AMF blends the Plogger Method with four chambers — Melody, Harmony, Voicings, Rhythm — taught simultaneously from day one. Plogger is the perceptual backbone: it gives students the language to hear intervals (di-chords), feel rhythm physically (lap map), and perceive modes. The chambers apply those perceptions to real music in motion. They are not sequential stages. They are parallel dimensions of the same musical reality.

The synthesis work is not about building translation tables between isolated systems. It is about recognizing that these systems are already teaching the same underlying truths through different lenses — and designing visual artifacts where multiple lenses converge into a single, richer view. A student looking at a great synthesis diagram should see something they already know, and suddenly understand it more deeply.

## Three Categories

**Category 1 — Enhancement:** An existing diagram from any source, enriched with visual or content elements from another source. The diagram stays itself but gains a dimension it was always missing. The result feels inevitable.

**Category 2 — Synthesis Original:** A new diagram for a conceptual space that multiple sources orbit from different angles but none captures together. Not gap-filling. Recognition that multiple systems are teaching the same reality — and the unified view that shows it.

**Category 3 — Moonshot:** No constraints. Jaw-dropping, mic-drop, never-existed-before artifacts. The litmus test: would a musician stop and say "wait, WHAT?" Would a teacher immediately want to print it? These may not look like diagrams at all.

**Evidence:** RC-06 provides the lyric-to-grid mapping with explicit correct vs. incorrect comparison; EM-08 Fast Analysis checklist uses the exact same logic (find the chord, is it a chord tone, place on map) that the lyric placement exercise implicitly requires.

**Source addition:** Rhythm Code
**Impact:** 9 | **Effort:** M

Neither source reveals this on its own — the pulsation table is acoustically abstract, and the clave grid is rhythmically abstract — but together they show the student that harmonic tension and rhythmic weight are the same phenomenon experienced through two different perceptual channels.

**Evidence:** RC-11 defines stops as rhythmically weighted note positions; Di-Chord Pulsation Table quantifies acoustic tension by Hz. The convergence of 'stop' positions and high-pulsation intervals is directly visible once the grids are aligned.

### S7. The Tritone Under the Microscope: One Interval, Four Systems
