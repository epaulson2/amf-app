Beyond the Clave

The clave has 5 notes. But there are 16 positions. What about the other 11?

The Rhythm Code maps ALL 16 positions across two measures. Three states: STRONG (ideal stop positions) · MEDIUM (occasional) · AVOID (weak positions for stops)

★
STRONG — ideal stop position. Clave notes and quarter note anticipation.
●
MEDIUM — acceptable. Occasional stops OK.
✕
AVOID — weak. Stops here sound awkward.

Key Insights from the Map

2-Side Structure

THREE strong positions on the 2-side: the two clave notes (beat 2 and beat 3) plus the quarter note anticipation at "and of 4." The 2-side is rhythmically rich.

3-Side Structure

TWO strong positions on the 3-side: the clave notes that are NOT the first beat. The first beat of the 3-side is usually anticipated — felt as beat 1 but arrived early from "and of 4."

Quarter Note Anticipation (↑)

Position 8 ("and of 4") in BOTH orientations is STRONG. This is the quarter note anticipation — the most powerful single position in the Rhythm Code. Set a stop here whenever possible.

How to Use the Map

1
Identify the clave orientation of your song (2-3 or 3-2)
2
Transcribe the main melody or bass line in the binary grid
3
Find all stops — overlay the Rhythm Code map
4
Move stops at AVOID (✕) positions one step to an upbeat
