The borrowing rule: only borrow chords that CONTAIN the mode's characteristic note. Borrowing a chord that lacks the characteristic note adds no modal color — it's just a regular diatonic chord that doesn't signal the mode you're borrowing from.

The Backdoor ii-V uses borrowed chords from parallel minor (Fm7 from Aeolian, B♭7 from Mixolydian/Aeolian overlap) to arrive at the major tonic. The chromatic bass motion F→B♭→C creates a warm, inevitable feeling that's softer than the traditional ii-V-I but equally conclusive. This is the parallel-minor equivalent of the ii-V-I journey.
