Why It Works — Two Approaches to the Same Destination
G7 → C: Traditional
Dominant arrives from a P5 above. The tritone (B↑ and F↓) collapse inward by half steps to C and E. Strong, direct, expected. The "authentic" resolution.
D♭7 → C: Substitution
Dominant arrives from a half step above. The bass slides down chromatically (D♭→C). Smooth, sophisticated, chromatic. Same tritone resolution — different approach direction.
"The tritone is the engine of dominant function — not the root. G7 and D♭7 have the same engine (B↔F), just pointed in different directions toward the same destination. This is why tritone substitution works: you're swapping engines, not destinations."
