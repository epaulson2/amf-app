The Harmony OS Color System in Action

Three categories — Green (Diatonic), Blue (Secondary Dominant), Red (Modal Interchange) — applied to real progressions and real songs.
GREEN
Diatonic
Naturally in the key. Seven chords from the major scale. Home base — stability, coherence, and the foundation of all harmony. No accidentals needed.
BLUE
Secondary Dominant
Non-diatonic dominant chords. Each contains a tritone pointing at a specific diatonic target. V7/[target] — creates brief tonicization and directed forward momentum.
RED
Modal Interchange
Borrowed from parallel modes (same root, different scale). Creates emotional contrast and depth — the "darkness dial" — without leaving the key center.

Real Songs — The Color System Applied
Mostly Green
"I'm Yours"
Jason Mraz
Almost entirely diatonic. The bright, simple, singable character comes from staying in pure Green territory. One Blue chord (secondary dominant) adds brief color.

Heavy Red
"Kiss from a Rose"
Seal
Extensively uses ♭VI, ♭VII — borrowed from parallel minor. The lush, cinematic, timeless quality comes from heavy Red borrowing creating constant emotional contrast against the major tonic.

Mixed Green + Red
"Diary"

Alternates between diatonic Green stability and borrowed Red chords for emotional depth. The contrast between the bright sections and the darker borrowed moments creates the emotional arc.
