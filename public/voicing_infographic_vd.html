Simple ≠ Beginner
Using 3-note guide tones is not beginner playing — it's an advanced choice. Bill Evans played thin. Keith Jarrett plays thin when he wants space. Only using thick chords shows you don't yet control your texture.
The Intermediate Plateau
Most intermediate pianists default to full chords all the time. Fluency means moving freely across the spectrum based on musical intention — not defaulting to maximum notes. Thin texture = open, spacious, modern. Thick = full, complete, rich.
The Real Skill
Advanced players don't just use MORE notes. They control how thin or thick the texture is in real time based on emotional intention. A sparse two-bar phrase with thin guide tones, followed by a full chord with a 13th, creates contrast and drama. Knowing when to use each — that's the skill.
