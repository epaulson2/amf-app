The Harmonic Acquisition Model

How to build genuine harmonic fluency — three components working together in a feedback loop, just like learning a spoken language.
♫
Component 1
Playlist
25–50 of your favorite songs
Your existing musical loves provide the emotional context. Harness attention you already have — the songs you know deeply and feel strongly about.
How: Build a playlist of songs you love and listen to obsessively. These become your analytical corpus. Don't start with theory exercises — start with music you care about.
𝄞
Component 2
Theory
The language to label what you hear
Analyze your playlist. Assign functional labels. Name the chords, identify the cadences, spot the borrowed chords. Theory is vocabulary — useless without context.
How: Transcribe or look up chord progressions from your playlist. Label each chord with its function (Green/Blue/Red). Identify ii-V-Is, secondary dominants, borrowed chords.
◎
Component 3
Immersion
Daily listening + playing the structures
Creates the feedback loop. Massive input of labeled, emotionally meaningful music builds pattern recognition — the same way immersion builds fluency in a spoken language.
How: Play through progressions daily. Listen actively with theory labels in mind. Hum bass lines. Sing chord tones. The goal is automatic recognition, not conscious analysis.
