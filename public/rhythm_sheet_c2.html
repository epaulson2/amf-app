The Clave Across Genres

The clave is hidden in the stops of melodies across rock, funk, pop, soul, metal, and jazz

The Hidden Clave

"Nobody plays the clave rhythm in 'Shape of You' — yet it's there."

The clave can be HIDDEN in the stops and starting points of melodies — not played explicitly as a percussion instrument.
You don't hear the clave itself. You feel its pulse. The stops of the vocal melody, guitar riff, or bass line cluster around the clave positions.

How to Find the Clave in Any Song — 3 Steps

1
Find the Stops
Transcribe the melody (or the most prominent instrument) in the binary grid. Mark all stops — the last note before silence in each phrase.
2
Note the Positions
Record which of the 16 positions those stops fall on. Ignore non-stop notes — only the landing positions matter.
3
Match to the Clave Grid
Compare your stop positions against the 2-3 and 3-2 clave patterns. In a well-written song, most stops will align with clave positions.

Starting Points Matter Too
The FIRST NOTE of a motif often lands on a clave position, not just the stops.

"22" (Taylor Swift) — first note of the chorus lands on a clave position.
"Get Up" (James Brown) — the vocal starts precisely on a clave position and announces the orientation.

Rule: Both stops AND starting points of phrases cluster around clave positions in well-constructed songs. Stops = where phrases land. Starting points = where new phrases launch from.
