Guide Tone System

Strip the 5th. Root + 3rd + 7th. Two positions, one rule — the minimum viable voicing for maximum harmonic clarity.
The 5th adds nothing unique to chord identity — it doesn't tell you whether a chord is major, minor, or dominant. The 3rd and 7th are the guide tones: they define quality and functional movement. Stripping to these two notes + root gives you a thin, sophisticated sound and makes voice leading even easier.
The Guide Tone Rule Through Cycle 5
Through Cycle 5 (ascending 4ths), just FLIP between Position 1 and Position 2 on every chord change. The 3rd becomes the 7th of the next chord, and the 7th resolves to the 3rd — smooth by design.

Texture Principle
Guide tones = THIN texture. Use when you want space, sophistication, and room for the melody to breathe. Adding the 5th back = FULL texture. Knowing when to use each is what separates advanced players from intermediate players — it's not about using more notes, it's about controlling density intentionally.
