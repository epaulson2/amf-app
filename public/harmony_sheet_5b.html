Chord Function & Cadences

Every chord belongs to one of three functional groups — and those groups follow a universal narrative arc from home to tension and back.
The Three Functional Categories (Key of C Major)
Function 1
Tonic
(Home)
Primary: I (Cmaj7)
I vi iii
I is primary home. vi is the relative minor — shares 2 notes with I, so it sounds like home but with a sadder tint. iii shares stable tones but is rarely used today as a tonic chord.
Function 2
Subdominant
(Departure)
Primary: IV (Fmaj7)
IV ii vii°
IV is the classic departure — adds color and motion away from home. ii is the modern, jazzier subdominant (darker flavor). vii° is a contemporary option used in pop and neo-soul progressions.
Function 3
Dominant
(Tension)
Primary: V (G7)
V vii°
V7 contains the tritone — the engine of harmonic tension. Creates the strongest pull back to I. vii° is the classical dominant substitute but sounds dated in modern pop contexts. V7 is the go-to.

The 4 Cadence Types
Authentic Cadence
V → I (or V7 → I)
The period at the end of a sentence
The strongest resolution in Western music. PAC (Perfect Authentic Cadence): V7 → I in root position with scale degree 1 on top. IAC (Imperfect): V → I but 3rd or 5th on top — slightly softer landing.
Plagal Cadence
IV → I
The "Amen" cadence — peaceful, gentle
The church cadence. Softer and more peaceful than authentic — no tritone tension driving the resolution. Common in gospel, hymns, and folk. Ends on a sigh rather than a full stop.
Half Cadence
any chord → V
The question mark — creates anticipation
Ends ON the dominant — pauses on tension instead of resolving it. Like a musical question mark: "something is coming." Often used at phrase midpoints before the answer phrase resolves fully.
Deceptive Cadence
V → vi (or V → ♭VI)
The plot twist — delays resolution
V seems ready to resolve to I — but lands on vi instead. vi shares 2 notes with I (C-E share with Am's A-C-E), so it feels like home but with a sadder tint. Delays the full resolution beautifully. V → ♭VI is an even more dramatic deception.
