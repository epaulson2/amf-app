Context Changes Everything

The same melody note can feel completely different depending on which chord is playing underneath it
The note RE is always RE — but what it feels like is entirely determined by the chord underneath it. Watch what happens as we play the same RE over four different chords.

The Takeaway
Always think chord + note together — never the note alone.

RE is the same pitch in all four rows above. It is the chord context that decides whether RE resolves, supports, anchors, or floats. This is why skilled melody writers think in chord-note pairs at every structural moment — not just scale degrees in isolation.
