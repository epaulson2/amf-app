Four Chord-Change Behaviors

When the chord changes under a held or repeated melody note, one of four things happens to its emotional quality
The chord is moving. The melody note is staying. This is one of the most powerful moments in any song — a single note whose emotional meaning transforms as the harmony shifts underneath it. These four behaviors describe every possibility.
Stay Sweet
Zone stays in 1 or improves
Z1 → Z1
The note was a chord tone in the previous chord and remains a chord tone in the new chord. Both chords share this note — it stays stable and warm through the change. No emotional shift — the harmony moves but the note is carried along safely.
Example
SO held through C → G change. SO is the 5th of C and the root of G. Zone stays Zone 1 throughout.
Use it: For stability at chord changes — keeps energy calm. Great for chorus sections that need to feel settled and confident.
Turn Tense
Zone worsens or becomes more active
Z1 → Z2/4
The note was grounded, but the new chord makes it a non-chord tone or an unstable degree without harmonic support. Sudden forward pull — a comfortable note becomes searching at the chord change.
Example
MI held through C → G7. MI is a chord tone of C (Z1). Over G7 it's a non-chord tone. Shifts to Zone 4.
Use it: To create sudden emotional urgency at a chord change. Extremely effective in pre-choruses and builds.
Float Free
Tension releases, note becomes supported
Z3/4 → Z1
The note was tense or floating outside the chord, but the new chord claims it as a chord tone. Suddenly the tension is released — the note that was searching finds harmonic support. This is the "ahhh" moment of harmonic resolution.
Example
FA held through C → F. FA is a non-chord tone over C (Z4). Over F major, FA is the root! Shifts to Zone 2.
Use it: For release of tension at chord changes. Letting the chord "arrive under" a tense melody note creates a beautiful harmonic exhale.
Sink Deeper
Tension escalates at the change
Z2 → Z4
The note was already tense, and the chord change makes it worse — it loses harmonic support and its instability becomes raw. Tension compounds — each chord change makes the note feel more stranded and urgent.
Example
TI held through G → Dm. TI is chord tone of G (Z2). Over Dm it's not in the chord at all — shifts to Zone 4. More bitter.
Use it: For escalating drama in bridge sections. But needs resolution — a melody that keeps sinking deeper needs a strong Zone 1 arrival to release.
