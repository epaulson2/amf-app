Secondary Dominants

Dominant chords built on non-diatonic roots — each one "tonicizes" a diatonic chord by placing a tritone-containing dominant just a P5 above it. Color category: BLUE.
Definition
A secondary dominant is any dominant 7th chord placed a Perfect 5th above a diatonic chord (the "target"). Named V7/[target] — "five-seven of [target]." Contains a tritone that resolves directly into the target chord, creating a brief moment of tonicization.

Resolving to MINOR chord

V7/vi: E7 ← Bm7♭5 because vi (Am7) is Minor
Why V7/vii° Doesn't Exist
The vii° chord is diminished — already maximally unstable and built on the leading tone. A diminished chord cannot be "tonicized" because there's no stable tonal center to point toward. Creating a V7 of vii° would require pointing tension at something that has no harmonic gravity of its own. Diminished structures are devices, not tonal goals.
