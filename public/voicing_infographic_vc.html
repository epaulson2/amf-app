Why This Works
When roots move in 4ths, adjacent positions minimize voice movement — notes travel by step (1-2 semitones) or stay put as common tones. This happens because the 7th of one chord is often the 3rd of the next chord in Cycle 5. Position theory makes this automatic: you don't analyze voice leading, you just follow the P3→P2→P1→P3 pattern and smooth motion emerges.
