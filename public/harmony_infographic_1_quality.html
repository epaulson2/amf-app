Interval Quality Progression

The complete spectrum of interval qualities — from diminished to augmented — and how they relate to each other through inversion.
Quality Ladders
For 2nds · 3rds · 6ths · 7ths
Augmented (aug)
Raised above Major
↑ raise 1 semitone
Major (M) baseline
The default quality
Minor (m)
Lowered 1 semitone
↓ lower 1 semitone
Diminished (dim)
Lowered 2 semitones
↓ lower 1 semitone again
For 4ths · 5ths · Unison · Octave
Augmented (aug)
Raised above Perfect
↑ raise 1 semitone
Perfect (P) baseline
No Major/Minor — skip
Diminished (dim)
Lowered 1 semitone
↓ lower 1 semitone
Note: No "Minor Perfect" — Perfect intervals have no minor quality. Lowering a Perfect creates Diminished directly.
The Tritone — The Crossover Point
The tritone sits exactly at 6 half steps — the halfway point of the octave. It has two names that mean the same thing:
Augmented 4th (Aug4) = Diminished 5th (dim5) = 6 half steps = C → F♯ / G♭
Inversion Pairs — Original + Inversion = 9
Flip any interval upside down — the numbers add to 9, the quality flips (Major↔Minor, Aug↔Dim, Perfect stays Perfect)
