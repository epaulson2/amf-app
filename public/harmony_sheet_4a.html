Chord Inversions

The same chord tones reordered — inversions create smooth bass lines, voice leading efficiency, and new emotional shades.
An inversion places any chord tone other than the root in the lowest (bass) voice. The chord name doesn't change — only which note is on the bottom. Notated as Chord/Bass (e.g., C/E means C chord with E in the bass).
Triad Inversions — C Major (C-E-G)
Position Bass Note Stability Notation Character
Root Position Root (C)
Most stable		 C Grounded foundation. Strong arrival point. Use for beginnings, endings, and strong beats.
1st Inversion 3rd (E)
Lighter		 C/E Smooth, elegant, slightly softer. Excellent for harmonizing scale degree 3 in the melody (creates seamless motion in the bass).
2nd Inversion 5th (G)
Least stable		 C/G Creates a P4 above the bass — contextually unstable. Used in specific contexts: cadential 6/4 (IV 2nd inv before V) or passing movement.
7th Chord Inversions — G7 (G-B-D-F)
Position Bass Note Stability Notation Character
Root Position Root (G)
Most stable		 G7 Full, strong dominant. Direct and powerful resolution drive.
1st Inversion 3rd (B)
Lighter		 G7/B Smoothes the bass line when harmonizing scale degree 7. Classic pop use: harmonizes B in the melody moving up to C (e.g., "Man in the Mirror").
2nd Inversion 5th (D)
Less stable		 G7/D Less common. The D in the bass weakens dominant drive slightly. Used for passing motion or pedal tone contexts.
3rd Inversion ♭7th (F)
Most active		 G7/F The F (♭7) in the bass creates a harsh m2 against the expected resolution. Creates maximum forward pull. Used in Bach chorales and dramatic jazz passages.
Three Functional Inversions You'll Actually Use
1
I/3rd
C/E
Softens the harmonization of scale degree 3 in the melody. Creates a smooth descending bass line: C → C/E → F. Commonly used in pop ballads for a gentler Tonic chord.
2
V/7th
G/B
"Man in the Mirror" — MJ bridge
Smooth harmonization of scale degree 7 ascending to 8 (B→C). The B in the bass rises stepwise to C — the most elegant possible bass resolution. Pop and gospel staple.
3
iii/5th
Em/B
"Shape of My Heart" — Sting
Most melancholic harmonization of scale degree 5. Em/B creates a descending bass line feel (G→Em/B→C/E). The B in the bass sustains before resolving. Deeply introspective character.
