The tritone engine: G7's tritone is B (the major 3rd) and F (the ♭7). B rises by a half step to C (the root of Cmaj7). F falls by a half step to E (the major 3rd of Cmaj7). The tritone doesn't just create tension — it physically collapses inward by the smallest possible motion. This is why ii-V-I is so satisfying and inevitable.
