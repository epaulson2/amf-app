Melody × Harmony Convergence

How AMF stability and Voicings OS tensions combine to create emotional intensity
Four Convergence Types — When Both Systems Pull the Same Direction
AMF: Unstable + VOICINGS: Avoid Note
Maximum Dissonance
The melody note is inherently unstable in the key AND clashes with the voicing. Both systems push in the same direction — away from resolution. Every listener leans forward.
Example: FA (rank 7) over Cmaj7
FA is the most unstable scale degree in C major AND it's the avoid note (4th) on Imaj7. Both AMF and Voicings OS say the same thing: get out.

AMF: Unstable + VOICINGS: Chord Tone
Caught by the Chord
The melody note is inherently unstable — it wants to move. But the chord claims it, providing harmonic support. Creates forward motion without collapse. The tension has direction and a container.
Example: RE (rank 5) as 5th of G7
RE naturally yearns and searches, but G7 holds it as a chord tone. The instability is real, but the harmonic floor is solid underneath.

AMF: Stable + VOICINGS: Available Tension
Stable Note as Color
The melody note is harmonically stable in the key, but it's not a chord tone — it floats above the harmony as an available tension. Stability prevents harshness; non-chord-tone status adds modern color. Expressive without risk.
Example: DO (rank 1) as 9th of Bb7 (bVII7)
DO is the most stable note in C major, but over borrowed Bb7 it floats as a lush 9th tension. Open, expansive, modern.

AMF: Stable + VOICINGS: Chord Tone
Pure Zone 1 — Home
Both systems agree: this note is exactly where it belongs. Maximum emotional resolution and sweetness. The "arrived" feeling. When the melody and harmony both say "home," the listener exhales.
Example: SO (rank 2) as 5th of Cmaj7
SO is stable in the key (rank 2, "strong") and a chord tone in the voicing. Both systems pull toward rest.
