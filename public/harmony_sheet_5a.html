Functional Harmony

The Harmony OS Color System — three categories that explain where every chord comes from and what it does emotionally.
The Harmony OS Color System
GREEN
Diatonic
Chords naturally in the key. Home base. Provide stability, coherence, and the foundation of all harmony. These are the 7 chords you get "for free" from the major scale.
BLUE
Secondary Dominant
Non-diatonic dominant chords. Each contains a tritone. Create directed motion toward a specific diatonic target chord. "V7 of [target]" — borrowed from tonicized keys.
RED
Modal Interchange
Borrowed chords from parallel modes (same root, different scale). Create emotional contrast without leaving the key center. The "darkness dial" — adds depth, drama, surprise.
