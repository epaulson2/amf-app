Left Hand Straddles

The left hand intervals that define your chord's harmonic identity — from the bass up.
Never octaves — they sound amateur and create no harmonic information. Use these straddles instead. Each one tells a specific harmonic story.
The Four Straddle Options
Root + 7 — The Core Straddle
Cmaj7 — interval of a major 7th. Most common in jazz and neo-soul.
1
7
Works on all 7th chords. The 7th quality (major/minor/dominant) defines the chord. Clean, open sound.
Root + 9 — Warmth & Openness
C7 or Cm7 — interval of a major 9th (octave + whole step).
1
9
The 9th is actually the 2nd an octave up. Works beautifully on dominant and minor chords. Adds warmth.
Root + 6 — Classic Sound
C6 — interval of a major 6th. Complete, settled feeling.
1
6
Classic, complete sound — heard in jazz standards everywhere. The 6th is the 13th an octave lower. Bright and resolved.
Root + 5 + 7 — Fuller Sound
Cmaj7 — add the 5th for a three-note left hand voicing.
1
5
7
1 + 5 + 7 or 1 + 5 + 9. Fuller texture for when you want a more complete sound in the left hand.
The Core Principle
The Root + 7th (or 9th) is the essential straddle — it defines the chord quality and establishes the harmonic foundation. The 5th is optional: include it for fullness, drop it for space. The ear hears the outer interval (Root to 7 or Root to 9) as the defining sound. Master these four options and you have complete left hand vocabulary for any 7th chord progression.
