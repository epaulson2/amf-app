Chord Tone (CT)
In the
Chord
A note that belongs to the currently playing chord — the root, major 3rd, perfect 5th, or 7th. The chord supports and "catches" this note. It sounds harmonically expected.
vs
Non-Chord Tone (NCT)
Outside
the Chord
A note that does NOT belong to the chord. The chord doesn't catch it. Depending on key stability, this creates floating color, grounded tension, or dissonant edge.

The Core Principle
The chord under the melody determines which notes feel grounded and which create tension.
Change the chord to C → F, and the same FA that was Zone 4 (Bitter) over C becomes Zone 2 (Tense/Supported) over F — because FA is now the root. The pitch doesn't change. The context transforms everything.
Zone placement is always chord + key degree combined — never one factor alone
