Anticipations

Notes that arrive early — creating forward motion and the characteristic feeling of groove

What is an Anticipation?

An anticipation = a note on an UPBEAT with NO NOTE on the following downbeat

The anticipated note FEELS like it's on the next downbeat — but it arrived early.
Key distinction: not every upbeat note is an anticipation — only if no note follows on the NEXT beat.
An anticipation is always a STOP — the phrase lands on the upbeat, then the next downbeat is empty.

Anticipations in Popular Songs

"I'm Every Woman" — Whitney Houston
The very first vocal note is an anticipation — arrives on the upbeat before beat 1. Sets the groove from the first breath.
"Get Up" — James Brown
The guitar part anticipates beat 3 consistently throughout. Signature James Brown upbeat-heavy feel.
"Beautiful Day" — U2
Quarter note anticipation in the piano. Beat 1 of every second measure is empty — arrived early from "and of 4." The strongest anticipation type.
