The V chord (E7) stays MAJOR even in minor context — this is critical. The major 3rd (G♯) provides the leading tone that pulls up to A, the root of Am7. Without the raised 7th (G♯ from harmonic minor), the resolution loses its pull.
"The ii-V-I is the engine. Everything else is decoration, extension, or variation. Master this one motion and you understand the engine behind 95% of Western harmony."
