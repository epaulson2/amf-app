Chord Tone (5th)
G7 claims RE as its 5th. Now harmonically supported — but RE is still an unstable scale degree. Grounded tension, leaning powerfully toward home.

Chord Tone (Root)
RE is the root of Dm — the most important note in this chord. Maximum harmonic support. Still an unstable key degree, but now RE is the anchor, not the outsider.

Non-Chord Tone
RE adds a minor 9th above Am. Dark, edgy color — the minor 9th has particular intensity over a minor chord. Unstable key degree + outside the chord = bitter tension.

The Essential Principle — Module D
Every important melody note requires two questions: What is its scale degree? And what chord is under it? Only when you answer both do you know its emotional zone — and know whether it's serving your intention.
