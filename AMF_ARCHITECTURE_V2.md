# AMF Architecture v2.0 — Mastery-Gated Curriculum
**Status:** Active redesign — June 11 2026  
**Replaces:** Original 12-sprint linear map  
**Principle:** Slow is smooth. Smooth is fast.

---

## The Founding Insight

The original AMF Year 1 map was time-gated: 12 sprints, one per month, one anchor song per sprint. The redesign is **mastery-gated**: you advance when you own something, not when the calendar says so. Some sprints take 3 weeks. Some take 6. That is correct behavior, not failure.

This shift has a second consequence: the unit of learning gets smaller. Instead of "learn I-IV-V-I this sprint," the question becomes "what is the smallest ownable harmonic unit?" The answer — arrived at by following the Plogger method to its logical conclusion — is a **single di-chord root movement**.

---

## The Core Principle: Di-Chord Movements as Harmonic Atoms

The Plogger system builds melodic ear from di-chords — individual intervals perceived as sound, not as notation. The AMF extends this one level up: the movement between chord roots is also a di-chord.

When the bass moves from I to IV, the root moves up a perfect 4th. That is a [5] di-chord. When it moves from I to V, that is a [7]. From I to vi is a [9] (up major 6th) or [3] (down minor 3rd). The **same perceptual vocabulary** built in Plogger Section 1 directly maps onto how harmony moves.

This means the Musical OS and the Harmony Chamber are not separate systems — they are the same ear trained at different levels. Every harmonic movement you drill is also a di-chord recognition exercise. Every di-chord you identify by ear is preparation for hearing harmonic motion in real time.

## Two-Tier Structure

Everything in the AMF divides into two categories:

### Tier 1 — Spiral Threads
Skills that begin small and deepen forever. You never finish these. Every sprint adds a layer. Progress is measured by increasing fluency and range, not by completion.

| Thread | Where It Starts | What It Grows Toward |
|--------|----------------|---------------------|
| **Di-chord ear** | [3] and [4] only — major/minor quality | All 11 di-chords → melodic context → harmonic root movements → Tracking Page |
| **Keyboard visualization** | Natural notes by landmark | Chromatic notes → fixed-do fluency → intervals → chords → all 8 clefs → eyes-closed at speed |
| **Longy rhythms** | ta, ta-te | All patterns including compound, dotted, syncopated → integrated with Rhythm Code |
| **Melody zones** | Zone 1 only (chord tones) | Zone 2 → Zone 3 → Zone 4 → chord-change behaviors → 5 Key Notes → Tiny Tension Arc |
| **Rhythm Code groove** | 8-position grid + stops + anticipations | Son Clave → groove development 4-step process → clave fluency in both orientations |
| **Lap Map / physical keyboard** | Begins Sprint 3 | Tetrachord formation → mode modulation → conjunct heptachord → silent practice anywhere |

**How spiral threads work in practice:** Each sprint, every thread advances by one step. The new layer is introduced in Week 1, drilled in Weeks 2-3, and tested in the sprint exit. It does not gate progression — but it must show measurable improvement sprint-over-sprint. Threads that stall get diagnosed (Three Causes of Error applied to theory work, not just instrument technique).

---

### Tier 2 — Mastery Gates
One discrete skill per sprint. This is the gate. You do not advance to the next sprint until the current gate skill is genuinely owned on both instruments, in multiple keys, at musical tempo, without hesitation.

**Two gate types run in parallel:**

**Gate A: Harmonic Root Movements** (one di-chord movement per sprint)  
**Gate B: Plogger Chapter Mastery** (one chapter group per sprint)

These can advance at different rates — if the harmonic movement gate is met in week 3 but the Plogger gate isn't, you keep working the Plogger material. The sprint doesn't end until both gates are cleared.

## Mastery Criteria — What "Owned" Actually Means

This is the most important definition in the entire system. It replaces calendar time as the advancement signal.

**A skill is owned when:**
1. Correct without hesitation at musical tempo (not "slowly and carefully")
2. Demonstrated on both instruments
3. Demonstrated in at least 3 keys — not just the anchor song key
4. Can be identified by ear in a song in real time, without preparation
5. Can be used expressively — not just reproduced academically

**Stage labels:**
- **Stage 1 (Discovering):** First contact. Errors expected and correct. Do not stop at errors.
- **Stage 2 (Consolidating):** Sometimes know it. Slow, deliberate repetition.
- **Stage 3 (Fluent):** Always correct, at tempo, without conscious attention.

A skill is not owned until it reaches Stage 3 by criteria 1-5 above. "I can do it slowly" is Stage 2. Stage 2 is not the gate.

## Harmonic Root Movement Sequence

One movement per sprint. Sequenced by perceptual simplicity — the most stable, most frequent di-chord intervals first.

| Sprint | Di-Chord | Interval | Primary Movements | What You're Learning to Hear |
|--------|----------|----------|-------------------|------------------------------|
| 2 | [5] | P4 up / P5 down | I→IV (opens), V→I (resolves) | The same interval — one reaches toward subdominant, one returns home. The resolution is [5]. |
| 3 | [7] | P5 up / P4 down | I→V (the reach) | Dominant motion — the farthest stable reach before needing to return |
| 4 | [2] | M2 up | IV→V (tension builder), V→vi (deceptive) | The smallest step up — tension that wants to release |
| 5 | [3] | m3 down | I→vi (darkening) | The relative minor shift — same notes, different gravity center |
| 6 | [9] | M6 up | vi→IV (emotional lift) | The inverse of [3] — brightness after minor |
| 7 | [4] | M3 up | I→iii (mediant) | The bittersweet third — neither fully major nor minor in feel |
| 8 | [6] | Tritone | V7→I (tritone resolution) | The most tense interval resolving — the full explanation of dominant function |

**How this connects to the 12+2 Progression Spine:**

The original progression spine (I-IV-V-I, I-V-vi-IV, 12-Bar Blues, ii-V-I, etc.) is still the long-term target. But progressions are now understood as **combinations of movements you already own**, not new units to learn. You don't learn I-V-vi-IV as a progression; you arrive at it naturally because you own [7] (I→V), [3] (vi←I), and [9] (vi→IV) as separate sounds.

By Sprint 8, a student who has genuinely mastered all 7 movements can assemble any diatonic progression by ear. The 12+2 progressions become proof exercises, not lesson content.

**The 12+2 Progressions — Now as Proof Points:**

| # | Progression | Movements It Combines | Earliest Possible Sprint |
|---|-------------|----------------------|--------------------------|
| 1 | I–IV–V–I | [5] + [2] + [5] | Sprint 4 (when [2] is owned) |
| 2 | I–V–vi–IV | [7] + [3] + [9] | Sprint 6 |
| 3 | 12-Bar Blues | [5] + [5] + [2] | Sprint 4 |
| 4 | ii–V–I | [5] + [5] | Sprint 2 (but don't front-load it) |
| 5 | I–vi–IV–V | [3] + [9] + [2] | Sprint 6 |
| 6 | vi–IV–I–V | [9] + [3] + [7] | Sprint 6 |
| 7 | I–IV–I–V | [5] + [5] + [7] | Sprint 4 |
| 8 | Modal Vamp | Single movement held | Sprint 2+ |
| 9 | Circle of Fifths descent | All [5] movements | Sprint 2+ (one segment) |
| 10 | i–IV–i–V (harmonic minor) | [5] movements in minor | Sprint 5+ |
| 11 | Andalusian: i–bVII–bVI–V | [2] movements down | Sprint 4+ |
| 12 | Minor ii–V–i | [5] + [5] in minor | Sprint 5+ |
| 13 | Classical cadence | [5] + voice leading | Sprint 8+ |
| 14 | Neapolitan / bII | [6] tritone motion | Sprint 8+ |

## Sprint Structure (unchanged)

The five-section daily practice structure is retained. It works.

| Section | Time | Purpose |
|---------|------|---------|
| 1 — Plogger Foundation | 10 min | Dedicated, isolated Plogger chapter work |
| 2 — Integrated Work | 18 min | 2-3 multi-layered exercises combining all active threads |
| 3 — Song Learning / Practice | 14 min | Anchor song as recognition and proof |
| 4 — Jamming / Free Play | 10 min | Structured improv using only owned materials |
| 5 — The Synthesizer | 8 min | All four chambers simultaneously |

**What changes:** Section 2 now centers on the sprint's target di-chord movement. Every integrated exercise is built around hearing, playing, and recognizing that one movement in multiple contexts.

## Anchor Songs — New Role

Songs are **proof and recognition**, not primary learning material.

**Old role:** Learn this song this sprint.  
**New role:** Find the [5] movement in this song. Hear it in 3 different places. Play along and feel when it happens.

This means anchor songs can be richer harmonically than the sprint's target movement. The student isn't learning all the chords — they're hunting for the one sound they're currently training. Other harmonic movements in the song are heard as "not yet" material. They'll return to those songs later and hear new things.

**Song selection criteria (revised):**
1. Must prominently feature the sprint's target movement
2. Must be worth spending a month with musically
3. Must be playable on both instruments at a simplified level
4. No country

**Provisional anchor songs for Phase 1:**

| Sprint | Target Movement | Anchor Song | Why |
|--------|----------------|-------------|-----|
| 1 | Intro — no gate | Ain't No Sunshine | Minor quality, Zone 1, simple 3-chord |
| 2 | [5] P4 — I→IV, V→I | Stand By Me | I-vi-IV-V: [5] movement appears clearly multiple times |
| 3 | [7] P5 — I→V | TBD | Needs a song where I→V is the primary harmonic event |
| 4 | [2] M2 — IV→V | 12-Bar Blues vehicle | IV→V is the defining movement of the blues form |
| 5 | [3] m3 — I→vi | TBD | Needs a song with prominent I→vi darkening |
| 6 | [9] M6 — vi→IV | TBD | Needs a song built on the vi→IV lift |

Songs marked TBD are open decisions. The right song for each movement has not been locked in yet.

## Year 1 Realistic Scope

With genuine mastery gating, Year 1 realistically covers:

- **Spiral threads:** All 5 threads running and meaningfully deepened
- **Harmonic movements:** [5] through [6] or [7] depending on pace — 5 to 7 movements owned
- **Plogger:** Chapters 1 through ~12 (di-chord system complete through harmonicity)
- **Melody zones:** All 4 zones fluent; chord-change behaviors introduced
- **Rhythm:** Longy complete; Rhythm Code + Son Clave fluent; groove development 4-step internalized
- **Songs owned:** 5-8 songs at Performance Ready level (from memory, at tempo, in front of someone, without stopping)

This is a more musical year than 12 rushed sprints. A student who exits Year 1 under this model owns something real.

## What Has Not Been Decided Yet

These are open design questions. Answers will shape the next build.

**1. The [5] / [7] question**  
I→IV uses [5] (P4 up). V→I also uses [5] (P4 up — same interval). I→V uses [7] (P5 up). These are inverses of the same interval but feel completely different harmonically. Sprint 2 currently holds both [5] movements (I→IV and V→I) on the premise that they share the same di-chord. The alternative is to give V→I its own sprint since the resolution is so important it deserves full slow-cook treatment. Not yet decided.

**2. Songs for Sprints 3, 5, 6**  
The anchor songs for the [7], [3], and [9] movement sprints are TBD. The right songs haven't been identified yet. Selection criteria are established (above); specific songs are not.

**3. Guitar and piano at different mastery rates**  
A student may hit Stage 3 on piano for a given movement while still at Stage 2 on guitar (or vice versa). Does the sprint gate require both instruments, or can you advance if one instrument is fluent while the other is consolidating? Not yet decided.

**4. How much Plogger per sprint**  
The Plogger chapter sequencing from the original build reference assigned roughly one chapter or chapter group per sprint. Under the new slower pace, that may be too fast for some chapters (particularly Ch.9-14, the full di-chord system, which is dense). Whether to slow the Plogger thread or keep the original chapter-per-sprint pace is not yet decided.

## Design Principles — The Non-Negotiables

These do not change regardless of how the curriculum is restructured.

1. **ii–V–I is a drill vehicle, not the harmonic spine.** The 14-movement set is the spine.
2. **Plogger appears twice every session:** isolated in Section 1 AND as perceptual lens woven through Sections 2-4.
3. **Hear / Sing Before You Play** in every exercise, every section, every sprint.
4. **Few exercises, multi-layered** — 2-3 per section, each serving 3-4 purposes simultaneously.
5. **The anchor song threads every section** — it is not a separate activity.
6. **"The framework disappears"** is the north star. Everything points here.
7. **Mastery over pace** — the sprint calendar is a guide, not a contract.
8. **No country** in repertoire or examples.
